📊 About This Dashboard

This dashboard visualizes real flight data with interactive Plotly charts. Click the buttons above to explore different analyses and learn about:

  • Confounding Variables: Why simple comparisons mislead
  • Selection Bias: What data you're NOT seeing
  • Simpson's Paradox: When patterns reverse in subgroups
  • Statistical Significance: Tiny differences, big datasets
  • Correlation vs Causation: Why you can't claim causation
🎯 Key Takeaway: Simple averages often mislead. This dashboard teaches critical thinking about data.

1️⃣ Cancelled Flights by Month

🚨 Selection Bias: February has high cancellations AND low measured delays. Why? Flights in worst conditions got cancelled - they don't appear in delay stats! We're missing the worst cases.

2️⃣ Aircraft Manufacturers Over Time

📈 Observation: Boeing and Airbus dominate. What changed between manufacturers tells a story about fleet modernization.

3️⃣ Flights to SFO by Carrier

💼 Market Share: United dominates NYC→SFO. Other airlines have minimal presence on this route.

4️⃣ Weather Impact on Delays

🌤️ Confounders: Weather correlates with delays, but confounders exist: bad weather in winter → holiday travel → more delays; bad weather → cancellations (survivor bias).

5️⃣ Carrier Rankings by Delay

⚠️ Warning: This ranking is MISLEADING! Different carriers fly different routes. A carrier that appears to have high delays might just fly harder routes. This is CONFOUNDING.

6️⃣ Departure vs Arrival Delay

✈️ Key Insight: Notice points BELOW the diagonal line. This means arrival delay < departure delay. Why? Pilots make up time in the air! Longer flights recover more delay, so comparing airlines by ARRIVAL delay is confounded by flight duration.

7️⃣ Simpson's Paradox: Time of Day Effects

🎭 Paradox: Overall, later flights have more delays. But when you look at individual carriers, patterns differ! Different carriers have different route mixes at different times.

8️⃣ Aircraft Age Impact

🤔 Correlation or Causation? Older planes show more delays. But can we claim old age CAUSES delays? Other confounders: older planes fly different routes, operated by different airlines, have different maintenance practices.

9️⃣ NYC Airport Comparison

📊 Statistical vs Practical: Differences ARE statistically significant (with 336k flights!), but are 1-2 minutes practically meaningful? Would you change airports to save 1 minute?
